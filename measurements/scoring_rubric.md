# Scoring rubric (provisional)

Scores should be assigned comparatively across weeks rather than as claims of absolute measurement.

## Scale
Use a provisional 0-100 scale.

- 0-20 = very weak / barely present
- 21-40 = low
- 41-60 = moderate
- 61-80 = strong
- 81-100 = very strong / dominant

## Candidate dimensions

### fear_intensity
How strongly fear, threat, risk, danger, or loss language appears.

- Low: risk or threat is present but not dominant
- Medium: threat framing is recurrent and shapes the reading
- High: fear or loss framing dominates the discourse

### superiority_intensity
How strongly discourse is framed through hierarchy, inferiority, superiority, replacement, or status comparison.

- Low: hierarchy or status framing is present only weakly
- Medium: comparison, replacement, or rank framing recurs across the discourse
- High: hierarchy, replacement, or superiority/inferiority framing dominates the reading

### delegated_agency_intensity
How strongly solutions are framed as outsourcing judgment, action, or control to external systems or agents.

- Low: delegation is mentioned only lightly or incidentally
- Medium: delegation is a recurring solution pattern
- High: externalization of action or judgment becomes the dominant solution logic

### exploration_score
How much discourse remains open-ended, curious, or possibility-seeking rather than closed and defensive.

- Low: discourse is narrowed, defensive, or closure-seeking
- Medium: some openness remains, but defensive framing is still visible
- High: discourse is strongly open-ended, curious, and possibility-oriented

### expansion_score
How strongly discourse pushes toward adoption, scale, acceleration, or growth.

- Low: little pressure toward expansion or scale is visible
- Medium: expansion appears as a recurring but not dominant tendency
- High: adoption, growth, scaling, or acceleration strongly drives the discourse

### fixation_score
How strongly discourse narrows into repetitive closure, obsession, panic, or single-track interpretation.

- Low: discourse remains flexible and not strongly locked in
- Medium: narrowing appears repeatedly but does not fully dominate
- High: the discourse is strongly repetitive, closed, and fixation-driven

### urgency_score
How strongly language implies immediate action, countdown pressure, or “too late” framing.

- Low: urgency is weak or incidental
- Medium: urgency appears repeatedly and shapes part of the reading
- High: immediate-action or too-late framing strongly dominates

## Rule
These scores are provisional sidecar measurements, not canonical observation fields.
