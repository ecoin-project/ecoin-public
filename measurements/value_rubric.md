# Value rubric (provisional)

## Principle

This file does not attempt to measure “value itself” as a single number.

Instead, it tracks provisional dimensions of value formation:

- what is claimed as valuable
- what may drive behavior
- what may connect to spending, adoption, or expectation
- what may persist over time
- what may coordinate people
- what may become extractive

These dimensions are experimental sidecar measurements, not canonical observation fields.

## Scale

Use a provisional 0-100 scale.

- 0-20 = very weak / barely present
- 21-40 = low
- 41-60 = moderate
- 61-80 = strong
- 81-100 = very strong / dominant

## Candidate dimensions

### claimed_value
How strongly the discourse presents something as useful, necessary, beneficial, or important.

Questions:
- Is the object explicitly framed as needed?
- Is usefulness repeatedly asserted?
- Is value claimed directly rather than inferred?

### behavioral_value
How strongly the discourse appears likely to change action, attention, time allocation, or decision behavior.

Questions:
- Does the discourse push people toward doing something?
- Does it increase urgency, adoption, or experimentation?
- Does it make passivity feel costly?

### economic_value_proxy
How strongly the discourse seems likely to connect with payment, purchasing, market expectation, implementation budgets, or measurable adoption.

Questions:
- Does it sound monetizable?
- Does it sound procurement-relevant?
- Does it align with obvious market or enterprise demand?

### persistence_potential
How likely the value appears to remain across weeks rather than spike and vanish.

Questions:
- Does the framing look durable?
- Is it tied to recurring need rather than novelty alone?
- Would it likely remain relevant after hype softens?

### coordination_value
How strongly the discourse helps align multiple actors toward a shared interpretation, priority, or direction.

Questions:
- Does it create shared language?
- Does it organize collective attention?
- Does it reduce ambiguity enough to coordinate behavior?

### extractive_risk
How strongly the value formation appears to depend on fear capture, dependency, compulsion, status pressure, or attention extraction.

Questions:
- Is the value sticky in a manipulative way?
- Does it intensify dependence rather than capability?
- Does it convert anxiety directly into compliance or spend?

## Practical rule

Early use should remain exploratory.

Do not treat high value scores as automatically positive.
Do not treat low value scores as automatically unimportant.

A discourse can have:

- high behavioral value but high extractive risk
- high claimed value but low persistence
- high economic value proxy but low coordination value

The goal is not moral judgment, but structured observation.

## Summary

Use this layer to observe value formation, not to declare final value.

Track:

- what is being framed as valuable
- what may move people
- what may persist
- what may coordinate
- what may extract
